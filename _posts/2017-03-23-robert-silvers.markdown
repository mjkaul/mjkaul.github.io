The great editor Robert Silvers died this week; may he rest in peace. He has been a model for me of diligence and editorial vision since I started reading *NYRB* ten years or so ago.

Many remarkable tributes to his life and work (categories that in his case were particularly inseparable) have been written and published in the days since then. Here are a few:

- [*NYRB*](http://www.nybooks.com/daily/2017/03/21/remembering-bob-silvers/) and [*New Yorker*](http://www.newyorker.com/books/page-turner/remembering-robert-silvers) writers remember Silvers
- A [short remembrance](https://www.theparisreview.org/blog/2017/03/20/robert-silvers-1929-2017/) by Lorin Stein
- An [interview with Ian Buruma](http://www.slate.com/articles/arts/books/2017/03/an_interview_with_ian_buruma_about_the_genius_of_bob_silvers_and_the_nyrb.html) about Silvers
- [Cass Sunstein, “What Democracy Needs in an Editor”](https://www.bloomberg.com/view/articles/2017-03-21/what-a-democracy-needs-in-an-editor)
- [William Grimes’s *NYT* obituary](https://www.nytimes.com/2017/03/20/books/robert-silvers-dead-founding-editor-new-york-review-of-books-nyrb.html)
- [A thorough obituary by Geoffrey Wheatcroft in the *Guardian*](https://www.theguardian.com/books/2017/mar/21/robert-silvers-obituary)
