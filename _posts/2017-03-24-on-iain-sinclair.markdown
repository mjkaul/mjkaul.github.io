Reading his recent *London Review* essay, [“The Last London"](https://www.lrb.co.uk/v39/n07/iain-sinclair/the-last-london), I felt the awe I always feel when reading Sinclair. The adjectives that come first to mind when trying to describe his style—*sui generis*, idiosyncratic, masterful—are a demonstration that I haven’t sufficiently applied lessons from this style to my own clichéd writing. Sinclair’s is a prose style that combines the dense, historical lyricism of Geoffrey Hill with the speed and dexterity of Frederick Seidel. I have to read him slowly to absorb the density before reading him at something close to the pace his style encourages, like an amateur jazz musician learning a bebop tune: I play it at quarter-tempo, then half; perhaps one day at full tempo.
